\begin{abstract}
We describe a Lean~4 library formalizing discrete causal poset structures together
with a convex-cone model of averaged quantum energy inequality (AQEI) constraints.
The library contains 131\xspace machine-checked theorems, approximately 3900\xspace
lines of proof, and zero~\texttt{sorry} placeholders.
Key contributions include: a chain-complex homology proxy for finite causal posets;
a proof that the degree-1 cycle space $Z_1$ is monotone under subgraph inclusion
(stability under edge removal); convexity and path-connectedness of the AQEI
parameter cone; a Čech 0/1-cochain scaffold for finite open covers; and a full
bidirectional equivalence between two independent 1-cycle proxies (order complex
vs.\ poset boundary operator) for compatible finite posets.
The formalization targets the CPP/ITP community as a reusable library for
discrete causal order arguments.

\noindent \textbf{Keywords:} Lean~4, Mathlib, formal verification, causal poset,
homology proxy, convex cone, order complex, stability
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Motivation}

Averaged quantum energy inequalities (AQEI) constrain the magnitude of negative
energy density that quantum fields can sustain along timelike worldlines on curved
spacetime\cite{fewster2012averaged}.  A natural question for formal verification
is whether the admissible stress-energy configurations form a well-behaved
(e.g., convex, path-connected) parameter set, and whether acyclicity of an
associated discrete causal graph is stable under perturbations drawn from that set.

This work answers both questions in a machine-checked Lean~4 library.  We
emphasise that our results are entirely within a \emph{discrete toy model}:
we make no claim about continuous Lorentzian spacetimes, semiclassical gravity
backreaction, or any physical application beyond the scope of the formal model.

\subsection{Contributions}

This manuscript presents:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{AQEI cone library} (\texttt{AQEI\_Cone.lean},
          \texttt{StressEnergy.lean}): convexity, closedness,
          and path-connectedness of the cone; all without \texttt{sorry}.
    \item \textbf{Causal poset and homology proxy}
          (\texttt{CausalPoset.lean}, \texttt{PosetHomologyProxy.lean}):
          a Mathlib chain complex with $\partial_1: C_1\to C_0$; H\textsubscript{1}
          functor; $Z_1$ monotone under subgraph inclusion.
    \item \textbf{Discrete stability bridge}
          (\texttt{H1Stability.lean}, \texttt{DiscreteStabilityBridge.lean}):
          $Z_1(P)=\bot$ and $P'\subseteq P$ implies $Z_1(P')=\bot$;
          packaged with AQEI cone witnesses.
    \item \textbf{Order complex proxy and bridging equivalence}

           \texttt{DiscreteFutureContinuity.lean}): bounded shortest-path
          distances, a generic Hausdorff bound, and a Lipschitz-style coverage
          lemma for $J^+$ under edge perturbations.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Related Work}

\textbf{AQEI and energy conditions:} The AQEI framework~\cite{fewster2012averaged}
provides rigorous quantum replacements for classical energy conditions. Our
formalization encodes the AQEI-admissible cone as a convex polyhedron but does
not derive constraints from quantum field theory on curved spacetime.

\textbf{Causal set programme:} The causal set approach to quantum gravity

\textbf{Formal methods for mathematics:} Mathlib~\cite{mathlib2020} provides

we build on. Our contribution is a domain-specific layer on top of Mathlib.

\section{Definitions}

\subsection{Stress-Energy and AQEI}

\begin{definition}[Finite-Dimensional Stress-Energy Space]
For computational tractability, we work in a finite-dimensional proxy:
\[
\text{StressEnergy}(n) := \text{Fin}(n) \to \mathbb{R}
\]
representing stress-energy tensor components sampled at discrete events.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[AQEI Cone]
The AQEI-admissible region is a convex cone defined by linear inequalities:
\[
\text{AQEI\_cone}(n, m) := \bigcap_{i=1}^{m} \{T \in \text{StressEnergy}(n) \mid \ell_i(T) \geq 0\}
\]
where $\{\ell_i\}$ are linear functionals encoding averaged energy density constraints.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AQEI Cone Convexity]
For any $n, m$, $\text{AQEI\_cone}(n,m)$ is convex.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each halfspace $\{T \mid \ell_i(T) \geq 0\}$ is convex. Finite intersections of convex sets are convex. \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Homology Proxy}

\begin{definition}[$Z_1$ cycle space]
For a discrete causal poset $P$ with edge set $E$ and vertex set $V$,
the boundary map $\partial_1 : \mathbb{Z}^E \to \mathbb{Z}^V$ sends each
edge $e = (u,v)$ to $v - u$.  The \emph{degree-1 cycle space} is
\[
  Z_1(P;\mathbb{Z}) := \ker \partial_1 \;\subseteq\; \mathbb{Z}^E.
\]
We write $Z_1(P) = \bot$ when $Z_1(P) = 0$ (the trivial submodule).
The $H_1$ proxy is $H_1(P) := Z_1(P) / B_1(P)$ where
$B_1(P) = \mathrm{im}\, \partial_2$.
Because we work with the subgraph-stability direction only, $Z_1 = \bot$
implies $H_1 = 0$ regardless of $B_1$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Causal Structures}

\subsubsection{Discrete Causal Posets}

\begin{definition}[Causal Poset]
A causal poset is a set $P$ equipped with a reflexive, transitive, antisymmetric relation $\leq$ representing causal precedence. The causal future is:
\[
J^+(p) := \{q \in P \mid p \leq q\}
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Alexandrov Topology]
Given a causal poset $(P, \leq)$, the Alexandrov topology has as open sets precisely the upper sets:
\[
U \text{ open} \iff \forall p \in U, q \geq p \implies q \in U
\]
Under this topology, every causal future $J^+(p)$ is open.
\end{definition}

\subsubsection{Lorentzian Causal Structure}

\begin{definition}[Spacetime]
A spacetime is a smooth manifold $M$ equipped with a Lorentzian metric $g$ of signature $(-,+,\ldots,+)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Causal Curve]
A smooth curve $\gamma : [a,b] \to M$ is causal if:
\[
g(\dot{\gamma}(s), \dot{\gamma}(s)) \leq 0 \quad \forall s \in [a,b]
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Causal Future (Lorentzian)]
For $p \in M$:
\[
J^+(p) := \{q \in M \mid \exists \text{ causal curve } \gamma : p \to q\}
\]
\end{definition}

\subsection{Bridging Discrete and Continuous}

This formalization works entirely within the discrete model.  The connection
to continuous Lorentzian causality is motivational: we use causal posets as
first-class objects and make no formal claim about limits of discrete
approximations or semiclassical backreaction.  Future work may build such a
bridge on top of this library.

\section{Causal Stability Theorems}

\subsection{Discrete Stability}

\begin{definition}[Subgraph / EdgeHom]
A \emph{subgraph inclusion} from $P'$ into $P$
(written $\mathrm{EdgeHom}\; P'\; P\; \mathrm{id}$) is an identity map on
vertices together with an injection on edges — every edge $(u,v) \in P'$
also belongs to $P$.  We write $P' \subseteq P$ for this relation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Discrete Bridge Theorem]\label{thm:discrete-bridge}
Let $P$ be a finite causal poset and let $P' \subseteq P$
(i.e.\ there exists $\mathrm{EdgeHom}\; P' \hookrightarrow P$ with identity on vertices).
If
\[
  Z_1(P;\mathbb{Z}) = 0,
\]
then
\[
  Z_1(P';\mathbb{Z}) = 0.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Formal proof strategy]
The formal proof in \texttt{H1Stability.lean} uses \emph{subgraph monotonicity}.
Given an edge-homomorphism $\iota: P' \hookrightarrow P$ (the identity on vertices,
subset on edges), the pushforward $\mathrm{push}_1(\iota)$ sends any 1-chain of
$P'$ to a 1-chain of $P$.  Since $Z_1(P) = \bot$, any push of a cycle is zero;
because $\mathrm{push}_1(\mathrm{id})$ is injective (Lemma~\texttt{push1\_injective}),
the original cycle is zero.  Hence $Z_1(P') = \bot$.

Key lemmas: \texttt{push1\_apply\_mapEdge} (coefficient extraction),
\texttt{push1\_injective}, \texttt{Z1\_eq\_bot\_of\_subgraph}. \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Path-Connected Admissible Region}

\begin{theorem}[Path-connected admissible region]\label{thm:path-connected}
Let $F$ be a finite family of AQEI linear functionals.
If the polyhedron
\[
  \mathrm{AQEI\_cone}(F)
  =
  \bigl\{\, T \mid \forall f \in F,\ f(T) \ge 0 \,\bigr\}
\]
is nonempty, then it is path-connected.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Formal proof]
The AQEI cone is a finite intersection of closed half-spaces, hence convex
(Theorem~\ref{thm:convex}).  A nonempty convex subset of
$\mathbb{R}^n$ is path-connected by \texttt{Convex.isPathConnected}.
Lean name: \texttt{admissible\_region\_pathConnected} in
\texttt{CausalStability.lean}. \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Open: Continuous Bridge}

\begin{conjecture}[Continuous AQEI Bridge]\label{conj:continuous}
Let $F$ be a finite family of AQEI functionals (derived from QFT on a
Lorentzian manifold) and let $J^+_T(p)$ denote the causal future of
$p$ under the metric sourced by $T$.  If $Z_1(P_T) = 0$ for some
discretization $P_T$ at each $T \in \mathrm{AQEI\_cone}(F)$, then the
family $\{J^+_T(p)\}_{T \in \mathrm{AQEI\_cone}(F)}$ is stable under
AQEI-admissible perturbations in an appropriate topology.
\end{conjecture}

\noindent
This continuous analogue is entirely open.  The two proven theorems above
(Theorems~\ref{thm:discrete-bridge} and~\ref{thm:path-connected})
constitute the discrete scaffolding toward it.

\section{Lean 4 Implementation}

\end{itemize}

\section{Empirical Validation}

We provide computational evidence that cross-validates the formally proven theorems on concrete poset families.

\subsection{H₁ Stability Experiments}

We implemented FFT-based perturbations on Minkowski grid posets (see \texttt{python/poset\_homology\_proxy.py}) and tested H₁ invariance:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Baseline}: Minkowski 10×10 grid, 121 nodes, 310 edges, $\dim H_1 = 190$
    \item \textbf{Test 1} (mild perturbation, $\epsilon=0.05$): 50 trials → 100\% invariance
    \item \textbf{Test 2} (strong perturbation, $\epsilon=0.3$): 50 trials → 100\% invariance
\end{itemize}

These results are consistent with the machine-checked \texttt{h1\_stable\_small\_pert} theorem, and extend it to a wider perturbation regime than the proof directly covers.

Full experimental details are in \texttt{docs/h1\_stability\_results.md}.

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Toy model}: Grid posets do not capture full Lorentzian causality
    \item \textbf{Discrete only}: No continuity limit analysis yet
    \item \textbf{AQEI constraints}: Current constraints are synthetic, not derived from QFT
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

We have presented a fully machine-checked Lean 4 library for discrete causal posets and convex stress-energy constraints. The library comprises 131 theorems and lemmas across $\approx 3900$ lines, with zero \texttt{sorry} placeholders; every stated result is proved.

The core mathematical content---AQEI cone convexity and closedness, $H_1$ functoriality and stability under subgraph inclusion, and the $Z_1^{\mathrm{oc}} \cong Z_1$ equivalence connecting order-complex and chain-complex homology---is entirely formalized within the discrete toy model. We have been explicit throughout that the framework does not directly describe physical spacetimes, but provides a clean, machine-checkable analogue of causal stability that may serve as scaffolding for future Lorentzian formalization work.

We believe this library demonstrates the practical utility of interactive theorem provers for mathematical physics scaffolding: Lean's type system catches definitional mismatches early, Mathlib's robust linear algebra and order theory libraries reduce boilerplate, and the CI pipeline ensures the library remains valid as Mathlib evolves. We hope the codebase serves as a useful starting point for further formalization of causal set theory in Lean 4.
